What are the risks of using a laptop without an external monitor or keyboard?
Using a laptop without an external monitor or keyboard leads to poor posture due to lack of ability to position monitor or keyboard independently.
This leads to back and shoulder pain for sitting in the same position for a prolonged time.
Physical fatigue is another common problem users face due to lack of flexibility to move their body causing stiffness in muscles and joints.

What ergonomic equipment can improve posture when working on a laptop? (e.g., laptop stands, external keyboards, ergonomic chairs)
Laptop stands: Raise the height of the laptop to eye-level to avoid bending the neck.
External keyboards and mouse to ensure elbows are at 90 degrees and wrists are straight.
Use ergonomic chairs which are adjustable in height so it that puts less stress on spine.

What adjustments should be made to monitor height, chair position, and desk setup for a healthier workspace?
Set up the monitor height to eye-level using a separate monitor or a laptop stand.
Use ergonomic chairs (if possible use one that has massage support).
Adjust desk height to allow 90 degree angle for elbows.


What are some daily habits that reduce the impact of prolonged laptop use? (e.g., movement breaks, posture checks, eye strain reduction techniques)
Make a habit to stretch every 30 minutes to relax joints and muscle.
Every 20 or 30 minutes look at something that is at least 20 feet away.
Adjust screen settings by reducing brightness.
