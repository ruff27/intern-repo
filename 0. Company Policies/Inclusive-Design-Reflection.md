Who are considered vulnerable populations, and what challenges might they face in digital spaces?
People with disabilities, neurodivergent individuals, and people with mental health challenges are considered to be vulnerable populations.

The existing challenges they face in digital include:
Getting overwhelmed by complex interfaces or systems
People with visual impairments struggle with poor colour contrast or small text
Fast-changing contents may trigger anxiety or overload to some people

What ethical considerations are important when designing or working with neurodivergent individuals? (Hint: Avoid overwhelming UX, respect sensory needs, ensure clear communication.)
Limit excessive animations or visual clutter
Offer reduced stimuli (such as dark mode or reduced motion)
Use plain and simple language, clear icons and consistent layouts
Consider neurodivergent users while laying out the design process

How can you make interactions and content more accessible for people with ADHD or Autism? (e.g., simple language, predictable navigation, reducing cognitive load)
Break tasks into little pieces with visible progress indicators
Minimise unnecessary text or visual clutter to reduce cognitive load
Use consistent structure
Allow for reminders, scheduling aids, and focus timers

How can we support neurodivergent team members in a professional setting? (Hint: Be clear in communication, respect different working styles, and allow flexibility.)
Practice clear and direct communication and avoid any vague communication methods
Respect sensory preferences
Allow flexibility in working settings such as flexible hours, remote work, mode of communication
