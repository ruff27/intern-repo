Roles in an Agile Team  

#### üîç Research & Learn  
- What are the **key roles in an Agile team**, and what does each role do?  
  - **Developers**  : Core members of the development team who take part in designign and developing software as well as contribue to sprints/issues.
  - **QA (Quality Assurance)**  : QA are embedded closely with development team as they define test cases, conduct automated and manual tests, and provide continous feedback to the developers.
  - **Product Manager (PM)**  : Project Manager Focus on the overall product strategy and manages the team backlog ensuring team delivers best possible product to customers to align with business purpose and goals.
  - **Agile Project Manager (Agile PM)**  : Agile PM plans, leads, and organises teams to deliver projects in iteration or sprints. They oversee projects at a higher level, manage budgets and coordinate across multiple teams.
  - **Other supporting roles (e.g., stakeholders, UX, customer support)**  : UX are often part of the development team that designs the backbone of the product to ensure they deliver user-friendly products through research and feedback by collaborating with developers and product manager.
- [ ] How do these roles collaborate in **Kanban vs. Scrum**?
- [ ] In Kanban, team members take on responsibilities based on their experience or current needs. They developers participate in continuous development. Team uses visualisation to find out pain-points and resolve issues.
- [ ] In Scrum, developers, UX, and QA attend daily stand-ups to update on sprints. Product owners bring in the priotity list to the development team while planning. The whole team has a shared sprint goal.
