What is Agile, and how does it differ from traditional project management?
Agile is a dynamic, iterative approach to project management allows to focus on continuous development, flexibility, and collaboration with team.
In contrast, traditional project management is a linear and sequential approach that strictly follows certain steps must be completed before moving on to the next one.

What are the core values and principles of Agile? (Hint: Check the Agile Manifesto.)
Main objective is to satisfy customer needs through early and continuous delivery of valuable software.
Be welcoming to changes even late in development to meet competitive landscape.
Team should regularly reflect on how to be more effective and adjust its approach accordingly.

What are the differences between Scrum and Kanban, and when is each used?
Scrum is iterative as the team works in sprints follows the plan-do-check-act. Scrum has specific roles defined such as product owner, scrum master, development team.
Kanban is continuous development. Team moves to next task as soon as one gets done. There are no specific roles for kanban.

Scrum should be used with projects that have clear goals defined and have predictable deliverable dates. 
While Kanban is suited for ongoing-work or maintenence work where the goal is to complete tasks as they rise.

Why does Focus Bear lean towards Kanban instead of Scrum?
Focus Bear already has their project set up which means it does not require sprint planning. Focus Bear is more about maintaining the workflow and work through issues as they rise.
